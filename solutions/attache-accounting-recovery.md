<p class="page-intro">Attaché has been part of the Australian business accounting landscape for decades. Many mid-sized businesses relied on it for inventory, payroll, and financial management. As support contracts lapse and the software ages, businesses face an increasingly urgent question: how do we get our data out?</p>

## The Attaché problem

Attaché Business Solutions has a long history in Australia — the software powered accounting and distribution operations at thousands of businesses. But the product has aged, and businesses face real challenges:

- The software requires specific versions of Windows that are approaching end-of-life
- Payroll module compliance has become uncertain as legislation changes
- The IT infrastructure needed to run Attaché is increasingly difficult to maintain
- Modern alternatives (Xero, MYOB, NetSuite) require a migration that Attaché doesn't make easy
- Years of financial history, inventory records, and payroll data are at risk

The biggest migration challenge is the depth of Attaché data. Transaction history, inventory movements, payroll records, creditor and debtor ledgers — all stored in formats that modern systems need help reading.
